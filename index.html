Built for CPU.
On purpose.

Most KV-cache work assumes you have a fleet of H100s. rayakv assumes you don't — and that you'd rather not. The performance target is the inference path, not the marketing slide.

The GPU isn't always
the bottleneck.

For long-context inference, KV-cache serving is often the wall. rayakv removes that wall on commodity CPU — no GPU required, no CUDA toolchain, no driver hell.

If you're running edge inference, on-prem deployments, or just tired of paying GPU rent for cache hits — this is for you.

About that 506ms outlier

Out of 838,716 requests, exactly one spiked to ~507ms. That's 1 in 419,358 — disclosed openly rather than hidden. Root cause analysis in progress. If your workload can't tolerate a single multi-hundred-ms tail event, ask us about the SLA tier before licensing.
